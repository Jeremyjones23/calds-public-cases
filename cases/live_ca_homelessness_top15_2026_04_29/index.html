Supervisor Brief

CalDS flags the top 15 source-listed nonprofit co-applicants or project partners in official California Homekey and Homekey+ award lists for possible waste, fraud, abuse, or mismanagement review. The reason is not that the current evidence proves wrongdoing. The reason is that the official award records show material public-money project exposure, and the official homelessness outcome context moved the wrong direction in several matched project geographies.

The lead score from the internal deterministic runtime was 12.81 / 100. That low score means the case is source-backed enough to brief, but still blocked by missing source series such as direct payment ledgers, provider-attributable outcomes, standard agreements, audit records, executive compensation records, and draw/payment histories. The sentinel decision was DOWNGRADE_FOR_REVIEW, so human review is required before any outside-facing conclusion.

What CalDS Found

  • Official award exposure: HCD Homekey and Homekey+ award lists name the 15 nonprofits below as co-applicants or project partners on project-award rows. CalDS ranked them by the full award amount attached to each source row. That is materiality exposure, not proof of direct receipt.
  • Outcome mismatch: Official California System Performance Measures show homelessness service-system volume increased in several matched geographies from the January 2023-December 2023 period to the July 2024-June 2025 period, including Los Angeles Continuum of Care (+9.3%), Alameda Continuum of Care (+15.5%), and Sacramento Continuum of Care (+24.2%).
  • Top review posture: The strongest current review questions are for Hope the Mission, Weingart Center Association, DignityMoves, The People Concern, and California Supportive Housing because each combines high project-award exposure with worsening official geography-level outcome context.
  • Key caveat: County and Continuum of Care outcomes are contextual. They do not prove provider responsibility without provider-level utilization, completion, discharge, cost-per-service, and outcome records.
